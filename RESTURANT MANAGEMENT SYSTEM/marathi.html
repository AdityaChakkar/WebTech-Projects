Marathi Food

pav bhaji

Pav bhaji is a spiced mixture of mashed vegetables in a thick gravy served with bread. Vegetables in the curry may commonly include potatoes, onions, carrots, chillies, peas, bell peppers and tomatoes. Street sellers usually cook the curry on a flat griddle (tava) and serve the dish hot.

misal pav

Misal pav is a popular dish from Maharastra. It consists of misal and pav. The final dish is topped with farsan or sev, onions, lemon and coriander. It is usually served with bread or rolls toasted with butter and buttermilk or dahi and papad. It is served as a breakfast dish, as a snack and also as a full meal.

vada pav

Vada pav, alternatively spelt wada pao, is a vegetarian fast food dish native to the state of Maharashtra. The dish consists of a deep fried potato dumpling placed inside a bread bun sliced almost in half through the middle. It is generally accompanied with one or more chutneys and a green chili pepper.

vej maratha

Veg Maratha a very popular dish. Cutlets or vade made from veggies are dipped in spicy gravy. You can have this with naan, paratha, roti or rice too. This is a little spice and very delectable.

panir masala

paneer masala is a slightly sweet dish of paneer,originating from the Indian subcontinent,in which the gravy is prepared usually with butter,tomatoes and cashews.Spices such as red chili powder and garam masala are also used to prepare this gravy.

bhakar vadi

Bakarwadi in Marathi & Bhakharwadi in Gujarati is a Gujarati & Maharashtrian traditional crispy, deep-fried, disc-shaped, sweet and spicy snack popular in western states of Gujarat and Maharashtra in India.
